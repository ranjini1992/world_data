Analytical Trail

This data was taken from World Values Survey (www.worldvaluessurvey.org) which studies changing values and their impact on social and political life. I chose important children qualities because they are different from usual factors like GDP, poverty, literacy rate and it can add some perspective to our knowledge of the world.

Interesting Insights

1. Tolerance and Responsibility are important child qualities irrespective of culture but Imagination is not.
2. Being thrifty is a more important child quality in Eastern countries than Western countries.
3. In China, religious faith as a child quality dropped drastically from 2000 to 2005.
4. Surprisingly, obedience is a more important child quality in the USA than in China.
5. Hardwork is not an important child quality in Germany, Poland and Nordic countries unlike what stereotypes suggest.

Manipulating Data

First I collected data of ten child qualities from WVS for four time phases in spreadsheets. Then, I matched them to their country code. I got the basic code of an interactive map from http://bl.ocks.org/jeremycflin/b43ab253f3ae02dced07 which visualized population because it had the Mercator projection of world which is familiar to most of us. Then, I added a drop-down list, legend and slider to visualise ten qualities in a span of 20 years.
